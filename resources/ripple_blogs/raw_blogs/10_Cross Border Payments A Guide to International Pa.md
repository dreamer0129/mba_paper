Simply put, cross border payments are financial transactions that occur across national borders. They allow individuals and businesses in one country to transfer money to an entity in another. That could include a US company paying a supplier in Germany, a migrant worker sending remittances home to family, or a shopper in Tokyo buying sneakers from an e-commerce site in London.

These seemingly simple transactions are often highly complex. Beneath the surface, traditional cross border payments pass through a web of intermediaries like banks, fintechs or payment service providers that perform currency conversions, regulatory checks and more.

A typical transaction begins when a payer initiates a payment through their bank or app. Payment instructions are then shared between the sending and receiving institutions, necessary currency conversions are executed, and the payment is routed through the multiple payment networks that connect the two parties. Increasingly, organizations are turning to blockchain-based cross border payments to streamline these flows for greater speed, savings and transparency.

Cross border payments can be conducted using different types of payment networks and transaction methods. These can vary depending on factors like cost, transaction purpose or size, personal preference, or which countries are involved.

ACH networks enable secure, cost-effective account-to-account electronic transfers. Their reliability means they are commonly used for recurring bill payments or B2B transactions. Funds sent using ACH typically take a few business days to settle and donâ€™t usually support real-time authorization which can deter retailers from using this method.

Wire transfers send funds electronically between banks, often via SWIFT or regional systems. They are ideal for large transactions but can be pricey and slow, especially across continents.

Credit and debit cards enable businesses to accept payments globally and many automatically handle currency conversion. Fees can be steep, but they are a staple for cross border e-commerce.

MTOs allow consumers to send money without a bank account. They are widely used for remittances and person-to-person payments.

Cryptocurrencies, stablecoins and blockchain-powered payment rails can offer near-instant, secure cross border payments, often with lower costs and fewer intermediaries.

The reasons for cross border payments are as diverse as the countries they span.

Person-to-person (P2P) payments include remittances, the practice of sending money to family or friends living in another country. For example, a worker living in the US might use a mobile app or an MTO to send part of their earnings to their family in the Philippines.
