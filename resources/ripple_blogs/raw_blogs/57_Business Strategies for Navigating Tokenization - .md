Unlocking Business Value

The growing interest in and examples of tokenization can be traced back to the wide range of benefits that blockchain technology enables. From tokenization of financial services and real estate to ownership and event ticketing and carbon credits, tokenization is helping to maximize business revenue through streamlined management, increased accessibility and greater liquidity.

One of the main benefits is greater transparency. Transaction data stored on a blockchain is immutable, fostering greater security of sensitive information and a more trustworthy ecosystem. For inefficient markets like the voluntary carbon credits market that suffers from opacity and double counting, this represents a major leap forward in credibility.

Tokenization can also improve access to an asset class by reducing traditional barriers to entry, lowering costs or asset prices and eliminating geographical boundaries. This enables companies to offer customers a wide array of real world assets that were once out of reach. For instance, a real estate project in one corner of the world can now be tokenized and made available to investors in the other, at a price point that is more accessible.

But the benefits of tokenization can only come to fruition when the markets for these tokenized assets become more liquid and have a strong secondary market.

Companies can further benefit from the operational efficiencies of tokenized assets on the blockchain including faster transactions, reduced management fees, and lower costs. As one example, the XRP Ledger (XRPL)â€”an open-source, decentralized, public blockchainâ€”was purpose-built for financial use cases and has enabled over 2.6 billion transactions to settle in real-time, error-free, for more than a decade.

Additionally, because of the decentralized nature of blockchain and distributed ledger technology (DLT), tokenized asset markets can be accessed 24/7/365 regardless of time zone or traditional market hours. As previously mentioned, blockchain transactions are immutable which dramatically improves the traceability of a tokenized asset. Each of these benefits have positive impacts on the bottom line or can be passed along to end-users to create a competitive advantage within the marketplace.

Industries at the Forefront of Tokenization

While tokenization will undoubtedly touch all corners of the world, certain industries are particularly poised to thrive in the emerging token economy. Bolstered by the foundational elements outlined above, first movers joined by a reliable crypto liquidity provider will help lead the charge in adopting tokenization.

As bank-issued stablecoins continue to gain traction, financial institutions will play a pivotal role in the payments ecosystem. Using blockchain to power a modern payments solution, stablecoins can enable merchant settlement, cross-border payments, treasury functionality and tokenized loyalty programs among other use cases with faster settlement times, lower costs and greater transparency.
